If your business deals in goods, Acronix Books tracks stock alongside your accounts so quantities and values stay in step.

## Items, groups and units

- **Items** are the things you buy and sell. Each item carries its **HSN/SAC code**, **tax category** and default rate, so invoices tax it correctly.
- **Item groups** organise items into categories (for example *Raw material*, *Finished goods*) for cleaner reports.
- **Units** of measure (pcs, kg, metre, box) define how quantities are counted.
- **Tax categories** group items by GST rate so tax is applied consistently — see [Tax setup](/docs/tax-setup/).

## Warehouses

Track stock across multiple **warehouses** (or godowns). Every stock movement is attributed to a warehouse, so you always know what's where — useful for businesses with more than one storage location or shop.

## Stock journals

A **stock journal** records movements that aren't a straight sale or purchase:

- Transfers between warehouses.
- Manufacturing consumption and production (raw material out, finished goods in).
- Adjustments after a physical count.

<Callout type="tip" title="Keep stock and books aligned">
Because inventory and accounting live in one app, a sales invoice both reduces stock and posts the accounting entry in a single step. There's no second system to reconcile.
</Callout>

## Delivery challans

Use a **delivery challan** to move goods without immediately raising a tax invoice — for example, goods sent on approval or for job work. Challans can later be converted to or linked with an invoice.

## Stock valuation

Acronix Books values stock by either **weighted-average** or **FIFO** (first-in, first-out). Set the default method per company and override it per item where a particular line needs the other. Every stock-in is recorded as a valuation layer, so you can switch method without rebuilding history.

## Job-work rates

For manufacturers and textile job work, **job-work rate** masters store the agreed rate for each process or job. This makes billing job work fast and consistent, and the **Job Work Register** keeps a clear record of material sent out and received back.

## Stock reports

Inventory feeds a dedicated set of reports — **Stock Summary** (quantity and value on hand), **Stock Ledger** (movement of an item over time) and **Party Stock** (stock held against a party) — alongside the Job Work Register. See [Reports & exports](/docs/reports-and-exports/).
