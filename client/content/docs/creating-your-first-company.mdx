A **company** in Acronix Books is a self-contained set of books — its own ledgers, vouchers, inventory and reports. You can create as many companies as you need and switch between them; each is stored and encrypted independently.

## Create a company

1. From the company screen, choose **New company**.
2. Enter the details:

| Field | Notes |
| --- | --- |
| Company name | Your registered business or trading name |
| Financial year | The start of your FY — India uses **1 April to 31 March** |
| GSTIN | Your 15-character GST number (optional if unregistered) |
| State | Determines whether a sale is intra-state (CGST + SGST) or inter-state (IGST) |
| Address & contact | Printed on invoices and reports |

3. Save. Acronix Books opens the new company and seeds a standard chart of accounts.

<Callout type="info" title="Financial year = April to March">
Indian statutory books run on an April–March financial year. Acronix Books uses this as the default reporting period for the trial balance, profit & loss, balance sheet and GST returns. You can open a new financial year each April without losing history.
</Callout>

## What gets created for you

To save you the tedious first-day setup, a new company starts with the standard Indian account-group tree, for example:

- **Assets** — Current Assets (Cash, Bank, Sundry Debtors), Fixed Assets
- **Liabilities** — Current Liabilities (Sundry Creditors, Duties & Taxes), Loans
- **Income** — Sales Accounts, Direct & Indirect Income
- **Expenses** — Purchase Accounts, Direct & Indirect Expenses

You can rename, add or reorganise groups from the masters section at any time. See [Invoicing & vouchers](/docs/invoicing-and-vouchers/) for how ledgers are used in transactions.
