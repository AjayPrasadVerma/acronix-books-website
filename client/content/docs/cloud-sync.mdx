Cloud sync is an **optional** feature that backs up your entire book to your own isolated space in the cloud and lets you restore it on a new machine. Acronix Books is offline-first: everything works without it, and you turn it on only when you want it.

## What cloud sync does

- **Full-book sync** — your complete book is synced, not just a subset.
- **Per-client isolation** — each client gets its own isolated, encrypted cloud backup, separate from every other client.
- **Fresh-device restore** — sign in on a new computer and pull your entire book down.
- **Offline-first** — you keep working with no internet; sync happens when you choose to connect.

<Callout type="info" title="Optional and reversible">
You don't need an internet connection to use Acronix Books. Cloud sync is something you opt into. Your local, encrypted book remains the source of truth on your machine.
</Callout>

## Turn on cloud sync

1. Open **Settings → Cloud sync**.
2. Sign in to enable sync for this book. Acronix Books provisions your isolated cloud backup automatically the first time.
3. Choose when to sync — you control when your machine connects and pushes changes up.

## Restore on a new device

1. Install Acronix Books on the new computer — see [Installation](/docs/installation/).
2. During onboarding, choose to **restore from cloud** and sign in.
3. Acronix Books pulls your book down and opens it locally.

<Callout type="tip" title="Moving to a new laptop">
Cloud sync makes device migration painless: enable sync on the old machine, sync, then restore from cloud on the new one. No manual file copying, and the transfer stays encrypted end to end.
</Callout>

## Security of synced data

Your book is encrypted before it leaves your machine, and each client's backup is isolated from others. See [Security](/docs/security/) for how encryption keys are derived and protected. For point-in-time archives you also control locally, combine cloud sync with [local backups](/docs/backup-and-restore/).
