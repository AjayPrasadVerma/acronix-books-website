The best Tally alternative for GST billing in 2026

Acronix Books Team1 January 19704 min read
GSTTally alternativeBilling

For a generation of Indian businesses, "accounting software" simply meant Tally. It is capable, it is everywhere, and every second accountant knows it. So why are so many businesses in 2026 actively looking for a Tally alternative — and what should they move to?

This post is an honest look at what to weigh when you switch, and where a modern, offline-first tool fits in.

What people actually want to replace#

When business owners say they want to move off Tally, they rarely mean the accounting engine. They usually mean one or more of these frictions:

  • The interface feels dated. New staff take weeks to get comfortable, and everyday tasks involve more keystrokes and menu-diving than they should.
  • GST work is fiddly. Generating GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B, reconciling them against the books, and exporting for the portal can feel like a monthly fire drill.
  • Setup and licensing friction. Installation, activation and moving to a new machine are heavier than they need to be.

The good news: you can fix these without giving up the accounting model you already know.

The non-negotiables for GST billing in India#

Any serious contender has to nail the fundamentals:

  1. Correct GST logic — automatic CGST + SGST for intra-state and IGST for inter-state sales, driven by the party's state.
  2. HSN and tax categories on every item, flowing into a clean HSN summary.
  3. One-click GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B you can reconcile and export.
  4. Proper invoice numbering via voucher series that reset cleanly each financial year.
  5. Reliable data — encrypted, backed up, and recoverable.

If a tool can't do these, it doesn't matter how pretty it looks.

Where Acronix Books fits#

Acronix Books is a modern ERP and accounting desktop app built specifically for Indian businesses of every size. It keeps the familiar Indian model — ledgers, account groups, vouchers and voucher series — so your muscle memory and your accountant's knowledge transfer directly. What changes is the experience around it.

It's fast and keyboard-driven. Interactions are tuned to feel instant, lists are virtualized to stay smooth past 50,000 vouchers a year, and almost every action has a shortcut. Data entry stops being a chore.

GST is built in, not bolted on. Set the HSN code and tax category once on each item, and every invoice taxes itself correctly. When it's time to file, generate GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B and export them to Excel for the portal or offline utility.

It's offline-first. Your books live on your own machine and work with no internet at all. There's no dependency on a live connection to raise an invoice — a real advantage when the network is flaky. Cloud sync is available when you want it, entirely optional.

It's secure by default. Your whole book is encrypted at rest with AES-256, gated by an encrypted login, with idle auto-lock, a tamper-evident audit log and role-based access. See how the security model works.

And it's free in early access — no per-invoice charge, no feature paywall on the core accounting, GST and inventory features.

Desktop vs cloud: an honest note#

Some Tally alternatives are pure cloud/SaaS. Those are convenient, but they tie your ability to invoice to a working internet connection and an active subscription. For a lot of Indian businesses of every size — shops, distributors, small manufacturers — an offline-first desktop app is the more dependable fit. You can read a fuller argument in why offline-first accounting matters.

How to evaluate a switch#

Before moving your books, run this quick checklist:

  • Enter a week of real invoices — B2B, B2C, and at least one inter-state sale — and confirm the tax splits are right.
  • Generate GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B for that period and reconcile against your sales figure.
  • Test a backup and restore, and if you'll use it, a device migration.
  • Try it with the person who'll actually do daily entry, not just the owner.

If those four go smoothly, the switch will too.

The bottom line#

In 2026, the best Tally alternative for GST billing isn't the one with the longest feature list — it's the one your team can drive quickly, that gets GST right automatically, keeps your data safe, and doesn't fall over when the internet does. If that sounds like what you need, download Acronix Books and try it on your own invoices this month.
